Accessibility

Not just for folks with disabilities

Try using a computer with bifocals.

What happens when you break your wrist or thumb skiing?

Section 508

https://www.section508.gov/

Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973

In 1998, Congress amended the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 to require Federal agencies to make their electronic and information technology (EIT) accessible to people with disabilities.

Accessible Web content

https://www.w3.org/standards/webdesign/accessibility

  • Alternate text for images
  • CC or Transcripts for audio/video
  • Tables can be read by screen readers
  • High contrast and no color-blind issues (and no seizures)
  • Possible to navigate without a mouse

Good accessible habits

Always provide alt text

Avoid tables for formatting, and code correctly if necessary

Use recommended accessibility checking tools

A few tools to try

  • https://color.a11y.com/?wc3
  • https://www.ibm.com/able/dynamic-assessment-plug-in.html

Or anything on this list: https://www.w3.org/WAI/ER/tools/

  1. Package files for translation

  3. (Optional) Generate output from source
  4. Publish translated output

Writing for translation

Use simple English.

Avoid contractions, slang, and idioms.

Be aware of "variables"

Be aware of conditional text.

Variables and translation

Don't add s or 's to variables.

For example:

Click Create to add <keyword keyref="clr"/>s.
Renders as:
Click Create to add Colors.

What the translator sees

Except the translator will actually get the following:

Click Create to add

s.

And in some other file: Color

Conditions and translation

When you create a translation, the smallest unit you should make conditional is a sentence.

Just because you can add a phrase in English, doesn't mean that how the grammar works in another language.
