Working with maps

A ditamap is how you organize your content.

There are two major types of ditamaps.

Map style

A map style ditamap is just a table of contents. You can add a title, but not much else. You can create a hierarchy of topics.

Used with non-PDF content, such as HTML output, or to reuse.

Bookmap style

Intended for use with books. Bookmaps contain a lot of metadata to create all the front and back matter of a book, such as titles, sub-titles, publisher information, copyright information. You can also add specific references for chapters, appendicies, table of contents, index, and so on.

There are other styles of maps, but 95% of the time you only work with bookmaps and maps.
