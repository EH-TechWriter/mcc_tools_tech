Video length

Keep it short and focused.

Most people will watch a 30 second - 2 minute video on how to do a thing.

Most people "don't have time" to watch videos over 5 minutes.

Making a video

Write a script.

Write a checklist of how to prep your system for the video.

  • Make sure your screens are 'clean' (watch your browser search window)
  • Make sure your data is prepared

Record screen captures separate from audio.

Better to record shorter clips to edit together than one long take.

Screen captures

Practice!

Double-check that you prepped your screen/system.

Watch your mouse movements to avoid jumpiness.

Watch scrolling speed.

You will be re-taking virtually the entire video at least once.

Recording Audio

  • Get a good mic
  • Always record your sound in the same place.
  • Do sound dampening things to avoid echo.
  • Click record, take a breath, then start speaking.
Middlesex Community College - Tools and Technologies for Tech Writers - Winter 2022

Editing

Once practiced, one minute of video takes about an hour to prepare.

Your first video, expect 2-4 hours per minute of recording.

Have at least 2-5 seconds between sections. This lets people absorb information.

Follow any company guidelines.

Translating videos

Getting audio translated is expensive.

Provide a transcript or captions.

May be able to get the captions translated without re-recording the video.

(You should provide captions anyway for accessibility.)
